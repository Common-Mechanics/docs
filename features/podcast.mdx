Every daily briefing includes a spoken audio version of the same stories. If you'd rather listen than read — during a commute, workout, or any time your eyes are busy — the podcast covers the day's leads and significant ticker items in the same editorial voice as the written dossier. The episode is typically ready shortly after the text briefing publishes each morning.

## Synchronised transcript

The podcast player displays a word-level transcript that highlights each word as the audio plays. This is useful in several ways:

- **Skim while listening** — the highlighted word anchors your eye so you can follow along without re-reading.
- **Jump to a passage** — click any word in the transcript to seek the audio to that exact position.
- **Read at any speed** — if you prefer reading, the full script is available without starting playback.

Word-level timestamps are produced by running the audio through a forced alignment model (WhisperX), which matches each word in the script to its precise position in the audio file. The result is accurate to the word, not just the sentence.

## Chapters

Each episode is segmented into named chapters corresponding to the individual stories it covers. You can:

- See the full chapter list before starting playback
- Click any chapter to jump directly to that story
- Watch the current chapter title update in the player as the episode progresses

Chapters are derived automatically from the structure of the script and are titled by the editorial team before publication.

## Autoplay

When an episode ends, the player automatically queues and begins the previous day's episode. This lets you chain multiple days of coverage without manual interaction — useful for catching up after time away.

## Playback controls

The player includes standard controls:

- Play / pause
- Seek bar with chapter markers
- Playback speed (0.75×, 1×, 1.25×, 1.5×, 2×)
- Toggle transcript panel open or closed

## A note on how episodes are made

<Info>
  Each episode is produced by an AI pipeline: a script is written and reviewed for factual accuracy, then synthesised into audio using Cartesia Sonic 3 voice technology. The script goes through two editorial review passes before audio generation, checking for accuracy against the source stories and editorial quality. The result is reviewed before the episode is published.

  Like all AI-generated content, the audio may occasionally contain minor inaccuracies or infelicities. If you notice a factual error in an episode, the source articles linked in the written briefing are the authoritative reference.
</Info>

## Finding past episodes

Each date's briefing page includes its corresponding podcast episode. Navigate to any past date — using the date picker or a URL like `aisafety.fyi/2026-03-14` — and the podcast player will show that day's episode if one was generated. Episodes are available for all briefing dates from the podcast feature launch onwards.
