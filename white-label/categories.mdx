Categories are the five editorial buckets that every story in your briefing is classified into. They appear as filter pills in the daily briefing, as URL slugs at `/category/[slug]`, and as the organizational structure for the five domain SME editors in the pipeline. Choosing categories that genuinely partition your topic domain — with minimal overlap and clear boundaries — leads to better story selection and more useful filtering for your readers.

## How categories are defined

Categories live in `config/tenant.yaml` under the `categories` key. The pipeline expects exactly five entries. Each entry needs four fields:

```yaml
categories:
  - id: safety
    label: Safety
    long_label: "AI Safety Research"
    infer_tags: [alignment, interpretability, evals, existential-risk, ai-safety]
  - id: policy
    label: Policy
    long_label: "AI Policy & Governance"
    infer_tags: [governance, regulation, us-policy, eu, china, global-south, policy]
  - id: models
    label: Models
    long_label: "AI Models & Capabilities"
    infer_tags: [frontier-models, capabilities, agents, benchmarks, compute, open-source, training, reasoning]
  - id: industry
    label: Industry
    long_label: "AI Industry & Business"
    infer_tags: [big-tech, startups, funding, acquisitions, labor, industry, developer-tools]
  - id: security
    label: Security
    long_label: "AI Security & Cybersecurity"
    infer_tags: [military-ai, cybersecurity, biosecurity, surveillance, privacy]
```

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `id` | Unique identifier used internally by the pipeline and as the URL slug. Must be lowercase, no spaces. Must match the `id` of the corresponding SME editor in the `editors` section. |
| `label` | Short display label shown in filter pills and category badges (e.g. `Safety`, `Policy`). |
| `long_label` | Full label used in headings, metadata, and the category page title (e.g. `"AI Safety Research"`). |
| `infer_tags` | List of tags from your tag taxonomy that map to this category. Used for automatic category assignment when a story has matching tags. |

## Tag inference

Tag inference is the mechanism that automatically assigns a category to a story when the story's tags overlap with a category's `infer_tags` list. During the produce phase, the AI assigns 1–4 tags to each story from your tag taxonomy. After produce, a mechanical step checks those tags against each category's `infer_tags` list to confirm or correct the category assignment.

For tag inference to work well:

- Tags in `infer_tags` should be mutually exclusive across categories where possible. A tag that appears in two categories' `infer_tags` lists will resolve to the first matching category.
- Keep your `infer_tags` lists comprehensive. Tags that don't appear in any category's `infer_tags` will rely entirely on the AI's direct category assignment from the produce prompt, without the mechanical confirmation step.
- The tags in `infer_tags` must be a subset of your `tags` list in `tenant.yaml`. Tags not in the taxonomy will never be assigned to stories.

<Note>
  The pipeline is designed for exactly five categories. This matches the five domain SME editors that run in parallel during Phase 2 of the build. Do not add a sixth category — the pipeline does not support it and the validation script will report an error.
</Note>

## Connecting categories to editors

Each category's `id` must exactly match the `id` of one editor in the `editors` section. The editor for a category is the Subject Matter Expert who reviews incoming stories for that domain during the triage phase. The connection is by matching ID strings — there is no separate wiring step.

```yaml
# Category definition
categories:
  - id: policy
    label: Policy
    long_label: "AI Policy & Governance"
    infer_tags: [governance, regulation, us-policy, eu, china, global-south, policy]

# Matching editor — same id value
editors:
  - id: policy
    name: "Policy Editor"
    expertise: |
      You are the Policy Editor — an expert in AI governance, regulation,
      and geopolitics. You track legislative developments, executive orders,
      international agreements, and institutional decisions across US, EU,
      UK, China, India, and the Global South.
      ...
```

The sixth editor always has the ID `spotlight` and covers non-text media content (podcasts, videos, demos). This editor does not correspond to a category — its selections appear in the Spotlight section of the briefing, separate from the five main categories.

## After editing categories

After changing your categories in `tenant.yaml`, run the sync script to regenerate `web/src/lib/categories.ts`, which is the frontend's single source of truth for category definitions and tag inference:

```bash
node scripts/sync-tenant-config.js
```

Then validate the full config:

```bash
node scripts/validate-tenant-config.js
```

The validator checks that all five category IDs have matching editor IDs, that no `infer_tags` are duplicated across categories, and that all tags in `infer_tags` exist in the `tags` taxonomy.

<Warning>
  Do not edit `web/src/lib/categories.ts` directly. It is overwritten every time you run `sync-tenant-config.js`. All category changes belong in `config/tenant.yaml`.
</Warning>
