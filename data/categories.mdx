Every story that appears in the daily briefing â€” whether as a lead or a ticker item â€” is assigned one **editorial category** and up to four **tags**. Categories define the broad domain a story belongs to; tags give finer-grained signal about the specific topic within that domain. Both are assigned by the editorial pipeline at publish time, not added retroactively.

## The five editorial categories

| Category | What it covers |
|----------|----------------|
| **Safety** | Alignment research, interpretability, model evaluations, existential risk analysis, AI safety techniques and tools |
| **Policy** | Governance frameworks, regulation, US policy, EU AI Act, China AI strategy, global south AI policy, international agreements |
| **Models** | Frontier model releases and capabilities, agents, reasoning systems, benchmarks, compute infrastructure, open-source models, training methods |
| **Industry** | Big tech strategy, startups and funding, acquisitions, labor and talent, developer tools, corporate AI moves |
| **Security** | Military AI and autonomous weapons, cybersecurity, biosecurity, surveillance, privacy, dual-use risks |

These categories map directly to the five domain editors in the editorial pipeline â€” a Safety Editor, Policy Editor, Models Editor, Industry Editor, and Security Editor each review the day's stories from within their domain before the Chief Editor makes final selections.

## Filtering by category on the site

Category pages let you view a filtered stream of briefings showing only stories in a given domain. To navigate to a category view, go to:

```
https://aisafety.fyi/category/[slug]
```

The available slugs are `safety`, `policy`, `models`, `industry`, and `security`. For example, `https://aisafety.fyi/category/policy` shows every policy story across all past briefings, in reverse chronological order.

You can also filter the daily briefing in-place using the category pills in the filter bar at the top of any dossier page. Selecting a category hides all stories from other categories without leaving the page â€” useful if you want to quickly scan a specific domain on a given day.

<Tip>
  Category filter state is preserved in the URL, so you can bookmark a filtered view and share it. For example, bookmarking `https://aisafety.fyi/?category=safety` opens the latest briefing pre-filtered to Safety stories.
</Tip>

## How categories relate to scoring

Category and tags also influence how the P(Doom) sentiment tracker works. The pipeline applies category-specific sentiment rules when assessing whether a story moves the AI safety landscape in a positive or negative direction. A new safety evaluation tool (`Safety` + `evals`) defaults to positive sentiment because measuring capability enables responsible deployment. A report of racing dynamics (`Industry` + `frontier-models`) defaults to negative because competitive pressure tends to compress safety timelines.

These defaults can be overridden by the editorial pipeline when a story's context warrants a different assessment â€” the category-based rules are a starting point, not a ceiling.
