The fastest way to stay current with the daily briefing is to subscribe in a feed reader. AISAFETY.FYI publishes a standard RSS 2.0 feed that works with any client — from desktop apps like NetNewsWire and Reeder to web-based readers like Feedly, Inoreader, and NewsBlur.

<Note>
  The briefing publishes once per day at approximately 8:10 AM Berlin time (CET/CEST). Your feed reader will pick it up within the hour, depending on its poll interval.
</Note>

## Feed endpoints

<CodeGroup>

```text RSS
https://aisafety.fyi/rss
```

```text JSON Feed
https://aisafety.fyi/feed.json
```

</CodeGroup>

Both endpoints return the same 30 most recent dossiers. Use whichever format your reader prefers — most modern readers accept both. The RSS feed uses the `application/rss+xml` content type; the JSON Feed uses `application/json`.

## What each item contains

Every item in the feed corresponds to one day's briefing. When you open an entry in your reader you'll see:

- **The day's headline** — a short title that captures the dominant story
- **Executive summary** — 2–3 sentences covering the top stories, with inline links to the source articles
- **Lead stories** — the 2–3 most significant stories of the day, each with a source name and one-liner summary
- **All stories** — the full ticker of ranked items covered that day, with links to the original articles

Clicking the item title takes you to the canonical briefing page at `https://aisafety.fyi/YYYY-MM-DD`, where you can read full briefings, filter by category, and navigate between dates.

## Adding to your feed reader

<Steps>
  <Step title="Copy the feed URL">
    Copy `https://aisafety.fyi/rss` (or `https://aisafety.fyi/feed.json` for JSON Feed).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open your feed reader">
    In most readers, look for an **Add Feed**, **Subscribe**, or **+** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste and subscribe">
    Paste the URL and confirm. The reader will fetch the feed and populate your inbox with recent briefings immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If your reader supports folders or labels, filing this feed under an "AI Safety" or "Daily" group helps it stand out from lower-frequency sources.
</Tip>
