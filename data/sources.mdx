Every daily briefing is built from 114 RSS feeds monitored continuously across nine categories. Sources range from specialist AI safety research blogs and think-tank policy feeds to major wire services and podcasts. The pipeline fetches all feeds within a 28-hour window each morning, filters for relevance, and runs the result through an editorial scoring process before anything reaches the briefing.

Each source carries a **priority weight** between 2 and 5. Higher-weight sources contribute more to a story's composite score — so a paper from the Alignment Forum on a given topic will rank higher than a passing mention of the same topic in a general-interest outlet. Weights reflect editorial judgment about how signal-dense each source typically is for the AI safety beat.

<Note>
  The source list is maintained by the Common Mechanics editorial team. Sources are added, removed, and re-weighted over time as the coverage landscape shifts. If you notice a significant source missing, you can reach out via the [About page](https://aisafety.fyi/about).
</Note>

## How priority weights work

Priority weight feeds into each story's **composite score**, which determines whether a story becomes a lead, a ticker item, or is filtered out entirely:

```
composite_score = (topic_importance × 0.4) + (article_importance × 0.6)
```

Topic importance is influenced by the source's priority weight — a story from a weight-5 source starts with a higher baseline than the same story from a weight-2 source. Stories from key organisations (Anthropic, OpenAI, METR, GovAI, MIRI, and others) also receive a score bonus on top of source weight.

This means the briefing reflects both the importance of the underlying event *and* the significance of who is reporting it — a distinction that matters when the same news is covered by both a specialist safety researcher and a general-interest wire service.
