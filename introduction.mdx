Aisafety is a daily intelligence briefing for anyone who wants to stay on top of AI safety without spending hours reading research papers, policy blogs, and industry news. Every morning, an automated editorial pipeline scans 114 curated sources — from the Alignment Forum to MIT Technology Review — ranks what matters, and publishes a structured briefing by the time you reach your first coffee.

You don't need a technical background to read it. The briefing is written for policy staffers, technologists, curious professionals, and researchers alike — people who care about where AI is going and what the risks are, but who want a clear signal without the noise.

## Who reads Aisafety

Aisafety is built for people who want to stay informed about AI safety without becoming full-time researchers:

- **AI safety researchers** who want a morning digest of what the field published yesterday
- **Policy professionals** tracking regulation, governance, and geopolitics across the US, EU, UK, and beyond
- **Technologists** building with AI who want to understand the broader risk landscape
- **Curious professionals** who read widely and want a reliable signal on one of the most important questions of the decade

<Note>
  Aisafety is built by [Common Mechanics](https://commonmechanics.io), a design studio working at the intersection of AI safety, tech policy, and public infrastructure. The editorial pipeline runs on Anthropic's Claude.
</Note>
